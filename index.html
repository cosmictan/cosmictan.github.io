About Me

Astrophysics · RF & Microwave · Antennas & Propagation · Astro-instrumentation

I am an early career researcher with dual PhDs in Electrical Engineering and Astrophysics. I have conducted interdisciplinary research spanning astrophysics, instrumentation, and engineering. My research focuses on astronomical instrumentation, electrical engineering with an emphasis on antenna array systems, designing passive devices, fibre positioning systems, detector characterisation, medical imaging, extra-galactic astronomy, and exoplanet related stellar rotation studies.

Current Research

My research focuses on understanding how dust affects observations of galaxies, modeling advanced multi-object spectroscopy instrumentation, and developing tools for detector characterization.

Highlights

  • Developed a CMOS detector noise characterization and gain calibration tool
  • Contributed to astronomical fiber positioning systems for multi-object spectrographs
  • Published papers on galaxy dust obscuration and instrumentation modeling
